Lesson 5: Renewable Energy Sources

SDG 7 – Affordable & Clean Energy Duration: 10 mins Level: Beginner

1. Lesson Overview

This lesson explains what renewable energy is, the difference between renewable and non-renewable sources, and why shifting to clean energy is essential for a greener future.

2. Learning Objectives

  • List at least three renewable energy sources.
  • Understand why fossil fuels are considered non-renewable.
  • Explain one advantage of solar or wind energy.

3. Key Ideas

  • Renewable energy comes from natural sources that replenish quickly, such as sun, wind, and water.
  • Fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas) take millions of years to form and release CO₂ when burned.
  • Solar and wind energy generate electricity without emitting greenhouse gases.

India is one of the world’s fastest-growing producers of solar energy, with large solar farms in Rajasthan, Gujarat, and Tamil Nadu.

4. Real-World Example

Many schools worldwide are installing rooftop solar panels to reduce electricity costs, teach sustainability and cut down their carbon footprint.

5. Try This Activity

Look around your neighbourhood (or check Google Maps). Can you spot any solar panels, wind turbines, or mini-hydropower systems? Note where you find them.

6. Reflect & Discuss

  • What prevents more people in your area from switching to renewable energy?
  • How could the government or community encourage clean energy adoption?

7. Mini Self-Check

🔆 Quick check: Name one renewable and one non-renewable energy source.

Speak your answer aloud — this helps you remember more effectively!
