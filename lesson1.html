Lesson 1: Climate Change Basics

SDG 13 – Climate Action Duration: 15 mins Level: Beginner

1. Lesson overview

In this lesson you’ll understand what climate change is, why it is happening, and why it matters for people and ecosystems. You’ll see how greenhouse gases trap heat and how this changes weather patterns around the world.

2. Learning objectives

  • Explain the difference between weather and climate.
  • Identify the main human causes of climate change.
  • Describe at least two impacts of climate change on people or nature.

3. Key ideas

  • Weather vs climate: Weather is the condition of the atmosphere on a short time scale (today or this week). Climate is the average pattern of weather over many years.
  • Greenhouse effect: Burning fossil fuels like coal, oil and gas releases CO₂, a greenhouse gas. CO₂ traps heat near the Earth’s surface and causes global warming.
  • Impacts: Climate change can cause stronger heatwaves, shifting rainfall, melting ice sheets and rising sea levels.

India example: Many parts of India are already experiencing more intense heatwaves and changes in monsoon patterns. This affects agriculture, water supply, health and daily life.

4. Real-world example

When temperatures rise and rainfall patterns change, farmers may lose crops due to drought or floods. Cities may face water shortages or flooding. Coastal communities can be forced to move inland because of sea-level rise and storms.

5. Try this activity

For one day, note down every activity you do that uses electricity or fuel, such as using a fan, AC, mobile charging, travelling by bus or bike, and switching on lights. Put a star next to activities you could reduce, switch off sooner, or do in a more efficient way.

6. Reflect & discuss

  • Which climate impact worries you the most in your city or region, and why?
  • Name one lifestyle change you can start this week to reduce your carbon footprint (for example: walking more, saving electricity, or eating less wastefully).

7. Mini self-check

🌍 Quick check: Name one greenhouse gas and one climate change impact you read about in this lesson.

You don’t have to type the answer here – say it out loud or write it in your notebook to lock in your learning.
