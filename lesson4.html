Lesson 4: Sustainable Cities & Communities

SDG 11 – Sustainable Cities & Communities Duration: 18 mins Level: Intermediate

1. Lesson Overview

This lesson explores what makes a city sustainable — including transport, housing, green spaces, pollution control, and waste systems — and why these matter for the health and well-being of people.

2. Learning Objectives

  • Define what a sustainable city is.
  • Understand how transport, buildings, planning, and waste systems affect sustainability.
  • Suggest improvements to make your neighbourhood or town more sustainable.

3. Key Ideas

  • Sustainable cities are safe, inclusive, resilient, accessible and low-carbon.
  • Public transport, walkability, and cycling reduce pollution and congestion.
  • Green spaces improve air quality, reduce heat, and support community health.
  • Efficient buildings and proper waste systems improve quality of life.

Cities like Pune and Bengaluru are investing in cycle tracks, metro expansion, and pedestrian-friendly zones to reduce traffic pollution and improve mobility.

4. Real-World Example

In Indore, India’s cleanest city for several years, strict waste segregation rules, community participation, and efficient waste collection have transformed urban cleanliness and reduced pollution drastically.

5. Try This Activity

Draw a simple map of your route to school. Mark where sidewalks, trees, streetlights, cycle lanes or safe pedestrian crossings could be added.

6. Reflect & Discuss

  • What is one thing you like or dislike about how your city is planned?
  • If you were the mayor for a day, which sustainable project would you start first?

7. Mini Self-Check

🏙️ Quick check: Why does better public transport help both people and the environment?

Say your answer aloud — it helps reinforce the learning!
