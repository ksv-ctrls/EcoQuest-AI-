Lesson 6: Waste Management Techniques

SDG 12 – Responsible Consumption & Production Duration: 14 mins Level: Intermediate

1. Lesson Overview

This lesson explains the principles of the 3Rs (Reduce, Reuse, Recycle), the different waste categories, and why proper waste management is essential for health and the environment.

2. Learning Objectives

  • Explain the meaning of Reduce, Reuse, Recycle.
  • Identify the main types of waste (organic, recyclable, hazardous, e-waste).
  • Suggest practical improvements to waste handling at home or school.

3. Key Ideas

  • The best waste is the waste that is never created — reducing is most important.
  • Segregation at source is necessary for recycling and composting to succeed.
  • E-waste and hazardous waste must be handled carefully to prevent pollution.

Many schools and campuses in India are becoming ‘zero-waste’ by banning certain plastics, encouraging reuse, and setting up composting systems.

4. Real-World Example

The city of Indore became India’s cleanest city by adopting strict rules for waste segregation, weekly awareness drives, and door-to-door collection with separate bins.

5. Try This Activity

Check your school’s dustbins. Are different waste items mixed together? Create a simple awareness poster that encourages proper waste segregation.

6. Reflect & Discuss

  • Which waste item do you throw away most often? Can it be reduced?
  • What realistic waste-reduction action can your class take this month?

7. Mini Self-Check

♻️ Quick check: Why is “Reduce” the most important of the 3Rs?

Speak your answer aloud — it reinforces learning!
