Lesson 8: Wildlife Threats in India

SDG 15 – Life on Land Duration: 12 mins Level: Intermediate

1. Lesson Overview

India is home to some of the world’s most unique and endangered wildlife. But many species face threats from habitat loss, poaching, and conflict with humans. This lesson explores the major risks and how communities and conservation groups can protect wildlife.

2. Learning Objectives

  • Identify at least two threatened species in India.
  • Explain how human activities lead to wildlife conflict.
  • Suggest ways to protect wildlife through community participation.

3. Key Ideas

  • Habitat destruction, poaching, and pollution are major threats.
  • Conflicts occur when animals move into human settlements looking for food or space.
  • Conservation succeeds when communities actively participate.

Note: Wildlife conservation is not only about protecting animals — it protects entire ecosystems.

4. Real-World Example

In parts of India, villagers receive compensation when wildlife damages crops. This discourages harmful retaliation and encourages coexistence.

5. Activity

Research one Indian endangered animal (tiger, elephant, gharial, rhinoceros, etc). Write down where it lives and why it is threatened.

6. Reflect & Discuss

  • Why protect wildlife even if you live in a city?
  • How can schools help spread awareness about endangered species?
