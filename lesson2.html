Lesson 2: Biodiversity & Habitat Conservation

SDG 14 & SDG 15 – Life Below Water & Life on Land Duration: 20 mins Level: Intermediate

1. Lesson overview

This lesson explores the variety of life on Earth (biodiversity), why it matters for humans and ecosystems, and how habitat loss and pollution threaten species. You will also learn how conservation efforts can protect habitats.

2. Learning objectives

  • Define biodiversity in simple words.
  • List at least two reasons why biodiversity is important for humans.
  • Explain how deforestation or pollution can reduce biodiversity.

3. Key ideas

  • What is biodiversity? It includes animals, plants, fungi and microorganisms, and the ecosystems they form together (forests, wetlands, coral reefs, grasslands, etc.).
  • Why it matters: Healthy ecosystems provide us with clean air, water, food, medicines, climate regulation and protection from disasters.
  • Threats: Habitat destruction, overuse of resources, pollution, invasive species and climate change can all reduce biodiversity and push species toward extinction.

When mangrove forests are cleared for shrimp farms or construction, fish nurseries disappear, shorebirds lose feeding areas and coasts become more vulnerable to storms and erosion.

4. Real-world example

In many coastal regions, mangrove forests act as natural barriers against cyclones and tsunamis. Where mangroves were preserved, nearby villages often suffer less damage. Where mangroves were destroyed, storms hit with full force, causing more loss of life and property.

5. Try this activity

Choose one local ecosystem near you (a lake, field, forest patch, park or even your school garden). Spend 15–20 minutes observing carefully and list at least 5 different species you see – including plants, insects, birds or animals. Note how they might be connected (who eats whom, who needs shade, etc.).

6. Reflect & discuss

  • Which species in your area seem to be declining or rarely seen now compared to earlier years?
  • What simple action could your school or community take to protect a nearby habitat (for example, a cleanup, awareness poster, or tree planting)?

7. Mini self-check

🦋 Quick check: Why is habitat loss such a serious problem for biodiversity?

Answer in your own words, speaking or writing, to help the concept stay in your memory.
