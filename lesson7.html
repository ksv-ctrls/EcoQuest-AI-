Lesson 7: Carbon Footprint Reduction

SDG 13 – Climate Action Duration: 8 mins Level: Micro-lesson

1. Lesson Overview

A carbon footprint measures the greenhouse gas emissions produced by a person, product, or activity. This lesson helps you understand how everyday actions increase or reduce emissions and how small changes can make a big difference.

2. Learning Objectives

  • Define what a carbon footprint is.
  • Recognize daily actions that increase or reduce emissions.
  • Identify at least two changes to lower your own carbon footprint.

3. Key Ideas

  • Transport, electricity use, food choices, and shopping contribute to carbon footprints.
  • Public transport, saving electricity, and reducing food waste lower emissions.
  • Small actions by millions of people collectively have massive global impact.

Did you know? Walking or cycling short distances not only reduces emissions but also improves health.

4. Real-World Example

Many households reduce their footprint by switching to LED bulbs, turning off appliances when not needed, and using public transport instead of individual vehicles.

5. Try This Activity

List 5 everyday actions from your week that increase your carbon footprint. Then write one low-carbon alternative for each—like using a fan instead of AC for mild weather.

6. Reflect & Discuss

  • Which habit of yours has the biggest carbon impact?
  • What is one easy change you can start today that you can maintain for a long time?

7. Mini Self-Check

🌡️ Quick Check: Name one high-carbon activity and one low-carbon alternative.

Think for 5 seconds — then say your answer aloud to reinforce learning.
