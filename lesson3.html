Lesson 3: Water Conservation Strategies

SDG 6 – Clean Water & Sanitation Duration: 12 mins Level: Beginner

1. Lesson Overview

This lesson explains why fresh water is limited, how households and schools waste water, and simple strategies to conserve water effectively.

2. Learning Objectives

  • Explain why water is a limited resource.
  • Identify common ways households waste water.
  • Suggest practical water-saving methods for daily life.

3. Key Ideas

  • Only a tiny fraction of Earth’s water is usable fresh water.
  • Leaks, long showers, running taps, and overwatering plants waste huge amounts of water.
  • Rainwater harvesting, fixing leaks, and mindful use save water efficiently.

Many Indian cities face summer water shortages. Buildings that use rooftop rainwater harvesting reduce pressure on groundwater supplies.

4. Real-World Example

In Chennai, water scarcity became severe due to reduced rainfall and over-extraction of groundwater. Schools that implemented rainwater harvesting managed to save thousands of litres per month.

5. Try This Activity

Track your water use for one full day — brushing, bathing, washing, cooking. At the end, choose one habit you will change starting tomorrow to reduce your daily water usage.

6. Reflect & Discuss

  • Does your home or school have a rainwater-harvesting system?
  • Which water-wasting habit from your routine can you realistically change?

7. Mini Self-Check

💧 Quick check: Name one structural solution (like rainwater harvesting) and one personal habit change that can save water.
